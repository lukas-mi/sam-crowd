The annotation task should be carried out in 5 steps one after the other:

  1. Briefly read the whole article to understand its general idea and at the same time work on identifying the MajorClaim occurrences.

  2. Inspect parts of the article in more detail to annotate the regular claims (ClaimFor/ClaimAgainst) considering their stance toward the MajorClaim.

  3. Look for statements that directly support or attack the regular claims. Mark such statements as Premise components and indicate the specific relations (Support/Attack) between each Premise and a regular claim.

  4. Consider statements that express relations toward the premises annotated in the previous step. Similarly to the previous step, annotate each such statement as a Premise and note the relation (Support/Attack) between it and another premise.

  5. Continue annotating and linking premises by performing step 4 until there is no statement that seems to be a premise that could be connected to some other premise.
